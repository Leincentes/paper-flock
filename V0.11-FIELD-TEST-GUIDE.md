# Paper Flock v0.11 — Field Test Guide

## Objective

Collect honest real-device evidence for responsiveness, blocked-path clarity,
rotation learning, sound, haptics, visual effects, and low-end performance.

## Creator setup

Host the project on any static web host or run it locally on the test device.
The participant URL ends with:

```text
?fieldtest=1
```

Use anonymous codes `TF-01` through `TF-10`. Do not use names, email addresses,
phone numbers, or social handles.

## Participant handoff text

> This is an unfinished puzzle game. We are testing the game, not you. Play in
> the way that feels natural. Small prompts will ask you to try sound and device
> feedback. Anonymous timing, performance, and survey data stay on your device
> until you choose to download the result. You may stop at any time.

## Required perspectives

| Perspective | Minimum |
|---|---:|
| Casual puzzle players | 4 |
| Experienced puzzle player | 1 |
| Casual non-puzzle player | 1 |
| Accessibility or sensitivity perspective | 1 |
| Low-end or older Android phone | 1 |
| Total protocol-complete sessions | 8 |
| Preferred total | 10 |

## What the self-guided flow does

### Natural-play phase

- Sound begins off.
- Effects begin on Auto.
- The participant plays without coaching.
- Tap latency and frame intervals are measured.

### Sound and haptic phase

After two levels or roughly two minutes, the participant is invited to enable
sound and continue for several moves.

### Low-end effects phase

Low-end sessions are invited to compare Lite effects. This ensures the
performance fallback is actually exercised.

### Final survey

After sufficient play, the participant evaluates:

- perceived tap immediacy
- blocked-path explanation
- neighbor rotation understanding
- movement feedback
- sound quality
- haptic quality
- distracting effects
- visible stutter
- instruction repetition
- critical barriers

## Session quality requirements

A protocol-complete record contains:

- completed final survey
- at least three measured tap samples
- at least sixty frame samples
- at least two minutes between session start and end
- tested sound when Web Audio is supported
- tested haptics when vibration is supported
- Lite or Minimal effects observed on a low-end session

Incomplete records remain importable but do not count toward the final evidence
gate.

## Importing returned files

1. Open the creator's normal game page.
2. Expand **Prototype testing tools**.
3. Choose **Import participant JSON**.
4. Select all returned participant files.
5. Check the field-coverage message.
6. Recruit the suggested missing perspective next.

## Acceptance criteria

| Measure | Target |
|---|---:|
| Tap response perceived as immediate | ≥ 90% |
| Blocked path correctly explained | ≥ 85% |
| Rotations understood by Level 3 | ≥ 80% |
| Movement feedback rated 4/5+ | ≥ 75% |
| Tested sound pleasant or neutral | ≥ 70% |
| Tested haptics pleasant or neutral | ≥ 70% |
| Effects distracting | < 15% |
| Repeated low-end stutter | 0 |
| Critical blockers | 0 |

## Decision options

- **READY FOR CLOSED ALPHA**
- **READY FOR ANOTHER CORE-FEEL ROUND**
- **REDESIGN BLOCKED-PATH FEEDBACK**
- **REDESIGN SOUND OR HAPTICS**
- **REDUCE VISUAL EFFECTS**
- **NOT ENOUGH EVIDENCE**

## Privacy

Review free-text responses before sharing exports because participants may type
personal information accidentally. Delete unnecessary raw files after the
decision is documented.
